who am i?

Hello! I'm Elva, an enthusiastic supporter of eco-friendly living and zero-waste fanatic. What started out as a curiosity in cutting back on plastic use swiftly developed into a way of life based on sustainability, mindfulness, and intention. My transition to a zero-waste lifestyle has changed not only how I buy and get rid of things, but also how I view the world and my responsibility to preserve it.

my experience with waste reduction

My first experience with eco-friendly living was introduced to me when my school hosted a zero-waste themed carnival in honour of Earth Day. A few of my friends were part of the organisation committee and indulged my interest to further my knowledge on this topic.

My first efforts to work towards a zero-waste lifestyle was to slowly get rid of bad habits and replace them with new ones. One of the old habits I replaced was only writing on the right side of a notebook, I now write on both sides by flipping the book 180 degrees to accommodate my wrist. My other efforts include recycling or donating old books and clothes instead of throwing them into the trash. Even after leaving the school that introduced me to the concept of zero-waste, I still continue to uphold my beliefs and continue to pursue a zero-waste lifestyle.

my favourite tried & tested 'green hacks'

Investing in a set of reusable bags

My set of reusable bags have protected my produce well and helped reduce my plastic waste. They're excellent for transporting other goods too.

Avoiding single-use plastics

Plastic cutlery and straws used to make up a large amount of my plastic waste. However, since owning a set of stainless steel cutlery and reusable containers, my plastic waste has been significantly reduced.

Switching to digital documents

My switch to digital documents has saved me not only on paper and ink but also time and money. Asking for paperless bills, and always opting for digital receipts has helped me become more organised as well.
